Planting and Care
Banana

Refer our Garden Guide for detailed procedures and meanings of words related to gardening. Sunlight: Morning sunlight of more than 6 hours a day is ideal for flower and fruit development. Soil: The soil should be well drained, fertile and rich in organic content for growing Banana plant. Watering: Poke your finger/plain small stick into the soil to check the moisture. Apply 4 cup (approx.200ml) of water when the top soil (1-2 inch) in the pot feels dry to touch. Do not overwater the plant. As a rule of thumb, water the plants thoroughly in summer and reduce watering in winter and rainy season. Water should be applied preferably in the morning or evening. Application of Fertilizer: Before application of fertilizer loosen the topsoil without disturbing the roots of the plant so, it can uptake the nutrients and moisture easily. Apply organic fertilizer once a month. Apply water immediately after application of fertilizer. Plant Protection: Remove dead, infected or damaged plant parts and discard them away from the plants. For any insect attack or disease, you can use Neem oil, Eucalyptus oil or Citrus oil spray for primary treatment.
